# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

## Phishing Awareness
1. Always check the sender's actual email address 
   carefully — fraudsters use addresses like 
   support@vit-bhopal.net instead of vitbhopal.ac.in
2. Never click links in emails or SMS directly — 
   always type the website URL manually in your browser
3. Look for HTTPS and a padlock icon before entering 
   any login details on a website
4. If an email creates urgency like "Your account 
   will be blocked in 24 hours" — it is most likely 
   a phishing attempt
5. Never enter your username or password on a website 
   you reached by clicking an email link

## General Cyber Safety
6. Use strong, unique passwords for every account
7. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all 
   important accounts
8. Keep your phone and laptop OS updated regularly
9. Never share your OTP or passwords with anyone — 
   not even bank or university officials
10. Do not download attachments from unknown senders

## Financial & UPI Safety 💰
11. Remember — to RECEIVE money on UPI you never 
    need to enter your PIN. If someone asks you to 
    enter your PIN to receive money, it is FRAUD
12. Never scan a QR code sent by a stranger claiming 
    to send you a refund or payment
13. Always double-check the recipient's UPI ID 
    before confirming any payment
14. Set a daily UPI transaction limit on your 
    banking app

## If You Are a Victim 🚨
- Immediately call Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930**
- Report online at: **cybercrime.gov.in**
- Change all your passwords immediately
- Inform your bank to freeze suspicious transactions
- Take screenshots of all suspicious messages
- File a complaint at your nearest police station
